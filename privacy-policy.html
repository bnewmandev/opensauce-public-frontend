Introduction
This policy covers the processing of personal data and how we use it. OpenSauce is committed to protecting the privacy of your personal information whilst striving to provide the very best user experience possible. We want our website to be safe and enjoyable for everyone. Under the Data Protection Act 1998 and related laws, we have a legal obligation to protect the personal information we collect from you.

If you have any concerns, questions or comments about this privacy policy or the website you can contact us using the contact details provided in the contact section.

What personal information do we collect and how
in order to make use of certain services provided through the blog such as commenting and writing blog entries, you will be required to make an account where the user will be required to provide information about themselves which is limited to a username and email. We will only use your information in accordance with your instructions and as set out in this privacy policy.

Demographic and other data
This website uses non-tracking cookies and weblogs to collect information about your use of our site, information about your computer such as your computer’s IP address and browser type, demographic data and if you arrived at our site via a link from a third-party site, the URL of the linking page. If you have registered an account with us, it may also collect your name and email address.

Email communications
We may keep track of the emails that we send you. We also keep a record of what communications you have sent us for a maximum of 5 years to ensure we are able to help in event of future communications.

How we use the information that we collect
All information gathered is owned by OpenSauce, the information provided will be used to do the following.

  • contact you regarding your registered account with us
  • monitor compliance with our terms and conditions of use of this website, enforce our rights and protect the safety of others including investigating and if necessary removing any content about which we receive a complaint
  • for any purpose, if we are required to do so by any law or other regulatory or government authority
  • send emails e.g password requests and security notices

By providing us with your personal information, you consent to us: (a) processing your information as set out in this privacy policy and in any other manner you expressly consent to your personal information being collected and stored, all personal information will be deleted if requested by the user, details on how to do so will be provided further down.

Storage and retention of data
Your information is collected and stored on our secure systems, mainly based in the UK and European Economic Area (EEA). Where we use third party service providers to store data, we have appropriate agreements in place to ensure that your personal data is protected. Your personal data may be transferred to a country outside the EEA if, for example, a supplier or service is based outside the EEA. We will take all steps reasonably necessary to ensure your data is treated securely, including ensuring the location has been granted ‘adequacy status’ by the European Commission, or using our agreement with the supplier contains model contractual clauses approved by the European Commission, or ensuring the company we are transferring the data to is certified under the EU-US Privacy Shield.

We hold personal data for a variety of different purposes and the length of time we keep your data will vary depending on the services or products we are providing. We will only keep your data for a reasonable period of time, which is based on the purpose for which we are using your data. Once that purpose has been fulfilled, we will securely delete that data or anonymise your information (so that we, or anyone else, can no longer tell that data relates to you) unless we are required to retain the data longer for legal, tax or accounting reasons.

How do I contact you
if you have any general questions about the blog or the information we have collected about you and how we use it, you can contact us via privacy@opensauce.uk.You are entitled to ask for a copy of the information we hold about you (for which we may charge a small fee) and to have any inaccuracies in your information corrected.
